About

Dr. Arturo Bouzas obtained his B.A. in Psychology at UNAM. He has an M.S. from the State University of New York at Stony Brook, where he worked with Howard Rachlin, Leonard Green and Gene Heyman. He obtained his PhD in Experimental Psychology from Harvard University, where he worked at the Pigeon Lab, advised by Dr. Richard Herrnstein.

Afterwards, he did a post doct in animal behaviour at Toronto University and, in 2001, he was a visiting professor at the University of California, San Diego.

He is currently a full-time tenured professor at the School of Psychology at UNAM, which he directed from 1997 to 2001. He is part of the National System of Researchers (second level) and currently holds the Ezequiel A. Chavez special chair.

Research interests

He's interested in the Adaptation of Behavior to the statistical properties of biologically important environments. His current lines of research include: Choice Behaviour, Behavioural Economics, Probability Learning, Intertemporal Choice, and Bayesian Cognitive modeling.
